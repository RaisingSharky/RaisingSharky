Classes

As you may have gathered, I don't really know what I'm doing when it comes to this whole child birth/rearing thing. Luckily, there's an app for that a series of classes which will undoubtedly fill me with both information and terror.

Show more

We've decided to go to the NCT courses based on advice from someone who's recently had a youngling. It's not the information that's most valuable, apparantly, but the people.
Lets face it, no matter how good your friends are, they're going to get tired of all your talk of nappies and baby sick pretty quickly so having some other sleep-deprived parent-zombies to relate to is definitely a good thing.

My only concern is that the courses have a bit of a hippy reputation and I'm expecting to have to share the room with a bunch of tree-hugging lentil-lovers...

The first appointment

Turns out American sit-coms aren't a good thing on which to base your expectations of pregnancy. Funny, that...

After we had a positive pregnancy test or three (the first pair were from Wilko's, we had to double-check with an expensive one), we figured a trip to the maternity clinic to confirm the pregnancy was the done thing. That's how it is in Friends, after all. As it happens, those sticks are so reliable that it's not worth a checkup, at least when the NHS is paying.

Show more

You don't actually get an appointment until 8 weeks into the preganancy, which feels like a long time to go un-advised when this fairly life-changing event is beginning. You don't really tell people until much later on in the little one's development so you can't get advice from people who've done this all before.
That in itself is a little odd. In that first few weeks we were both going through the excitement, trepidation and whatnot of getting the news while, all our nearest and dearest were oblivious. When it does come round to the big reveal to the family, you've pretty much got your head around things but they're all in that initial 'whoah...' phase.

I say I've got my head around it, but every so often it dawns on me that by the new year, a little person will be here that's entirely dependant on my wife and I.

Actually, that's pretty scary...

What's in a name?

My son has had many nicknames as he's grown from a Speck in the womb, through an entire salad of fruit & veg that the books use for size comparisons, and finally to Sharky. An odd name, you may think, but his flipping and wiggling in the last months before birth were more than a little reminiscent of a shark in it's egg-case.

The blog then, is named in his honour and as my wife and I don't really want photos of our boy all over the internet (they'd spread like wildfire - he's like a chubby Chris Evans crossed with a big-eyed Chris Hemsworth) I've decided to accompany the posts with doodles instead. Now, humans have never been part of my very limited artistic repertoir, but sea creatures I can do... sort of.


So, here we go, take a look at the blog page and you'll find what I hope will be a humerous account of me trying to cope with my son growing up.

Please feel free to share the posts using the social media buttons and, if I manage to implement it, you can pose questions using a link I'll add to the navigation menu.
